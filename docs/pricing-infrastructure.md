# Pricing Infrastructure

> Before a market can "discover" a price, engineering must first manufacture the "priceable object."

## Why Traditional Assets Don't Need It

Land, commodities, machines — pricing infrastructure exists naturally. A plot has location, area, amenities — self-evident to market participants. Novel assets are different: a low-altitude route has no location, no usable life. When there is no consensus on "what it is," price cannot be "discovered."

## The Dual-Layer Structure

| Dimension | Asset Pricing Layer | Risk Pricing Layer |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Core Question | What is this worth? | What could go wrong? |
| Carrier | LightSky Engine | RouteGuard |
| Method | 4-layer existence check → FVR-TVC → Monte Carlo | 4D risk factors → 5000 simulations |
| Output | ROI, NPV, payback period | Risk score, risk premium, actuarial rate |
| Downstream | → FLT translation | → YH → ILS bond pricing |

## Asset Pricing: LightSky Engine

**Four-layer existence check:** Airspace (gridded?), Technical (vertiport ready?), Economic (FVR ≤ 1?), Regulatory (approved?). A ∧ T ∧ E ∧ R = 1 → asset exists. Any = 0 → stop. This is code logic, not metaphor.

## Risk Pricing: RouteGuard

Proactive actuarial replaces retrospective actuarial. Four risk dimensions (geographic, meteorological, airspace, operational) → Monte Carlo 5000 runs → risk score distribution. No claims history required.

## Definitional Power

Whoever builds the pricing infrastructure defines how the asset is priced. Not monopoly — the natural power of the first to fill the vacuum.
