# Spacetime Analytic Right

> The fundamental power in the low-altitude economy lies not in the occupation of physical space, but in the capacity to segment, calibrate, and dynamically describe four-dimensional spacetime.

## The Two-Layer Structure

### Layer 1: Analytic Right

Answers the question: "What is this airspace like?"

1. **Gridding** — segment continuous airspace into discrete units. Precision is determined by Beidou/GNSS coverage.
2. **Time-slot allocation** — segment continuous time into departure slots. Granularity is determined by 5G-A communication latency.
3. **Attribute calibration** — attach communication coverage, navigation accuracy, meteorological risk, terrain obstacles to each spacetime unit.
4. **Dynamic updating** — reflect real-time changes: weather shifts, temporary no-fly zones, congestion alerts.

### Layer 2: Access Right

Answers the question: "Who can enter, and at what price?"

1. **Negative list** — designating prohibited areas and entry conditions.
2. **Differentiated entry** — different aircraft types and mission types receive different entry rules.
3. **Price signals** — different spacetime units priced differently based on analytic right calibration. Higher meteorological risk = lower price. Better communication coverage = higher price.

## The Formula

> Precision of Analytic Right = Vision of the Low-Altitude Economy

At low resolution, airspace control can only say: "this entire area is off-limits." At high resolution, airspace control can say: "this specific sector, at this altitude band, during this time window, under these communication conditions — you may fly."

Technical capability directly translates into: regulatory precision → entry flexibility → pricing differentiation.

## The Evolution of Spacetime Definers

| Version | Era | Power | Character |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| v1.0 | Land (Roads) | Land power | Universal but slow |
| v1.2 | Sea (Shipping) | Sea power | Special — only coastal nations |
| v1.7 | Air (Aviation) | Air supremacy | Elite speed — expensive |
| v2.0 | Low-Altitude (Drones) | Spacetime Analytic Right | Universal fast — cheap enough for every county |

v2.0 does not pursue "faster." It pursues "fast enough, and cheap enough to be universally available." When ton-kilometer cost drops to ¥2, the threshold drops from luxury goods to all mid-to-high-end consumer goods.

## Duality with Data Analytic Right

- Data Analytic Right cleaves the event of "data-to-insight mapping." Closed on the engine log.
- Spacetime Analytic Right cleaves the event of "origin-to-destination speed differential." Closed on the airspace slot pool.

One is a reputation good (each use adds an audit layer, increasing value). The other is a usage good (each takeoff consumes an irreplaceable time window, like flight slots).
